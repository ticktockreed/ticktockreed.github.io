Why do we have brands?

Traditionally products and services were sold by individuals on a one to one basis. Buyers would know sellers at a human level and understand their character, ideals and ability to deliver quality products and services.

It was a relationship built on trust.

The need for a brand arose from the requirement to allow a customer to know who they are buying from without ever meeting the owner of the company.

For all businesses, large or small, a constistent focus on building personal relationships with customers will be an essential ingredient for lasting success in decades to come.

The Human Brand

During, and post industrial revolution, companies were unable to continue the two-way communication between buyer and seller. Consumers lost their voice, and the era of mass-marketing was born.

Because of this, companies needed a strong identity. Starting out as a name and growing to include a logo, brand guidelines, tone of voice, print, tv advertisements and more.

This one-way communication from company to customer has been the standard for decades.

Times have changed though.

The two-way communication between company and customer has opened up again.
